## Extra (Added Thoughts)

A couple of things that might actually help you become "irreplaceable" in the time you have left, since you asked how to proceed:

- **500+ rules is a number, not a story.** Anyone reading your work later just sees rule count. What makes you irreplaceable is documenting *why* you tuned things the way you did — the false-positive patterns you found, the log sources that were noisy, the logic behind specific correlations. That judgment is much harder to hand off than the rules themselves.
- **Turn the red team testing phase into your biggest asset.** This is the part where you can show cause-and-effect: "I built X, red team tried Y, it caught/missed it, so I changed it to Z." That narrative is exactly the kind of insight that makes someone see you as someone who understands *detection*, not just someone who writes rules.
- **Consider writing a short handover/knowledge doc before you leave** — not because you're expected to, but because it silently proves your value. If Karthik sees a document that captures tuning logic, edge cases, and lessons learned that only you know, it does more to make you feel irreplaceable than anything you could say out loud.
- Appreciation from a boss doesn't always show up as praise in the moment — sometimes it shows up later, when they realize how much easier things were with you around. Don't let the lack of visible appreciation right now make you doubt the actual value of the work you've done.
