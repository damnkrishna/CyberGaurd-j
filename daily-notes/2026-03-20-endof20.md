# Turning 20 — A Reflection

## Who I Am Right Now

I don't know how to live alone yet. I don't know how to keep going every day, no matter what. I stop after two months, take a break for a month, then restart — but I can't afford to do that anymore. I'm an adult now. I have responsibilities.

I am still a confused kid, unaware of my life's purpose, unaware of what I'm really doing. I've been chasing other people's lives — comparing myself to others, getting inspired by reels and movies for a day or two, then feeling lost again.

That's what I've done my whole life — trying to be someone I'm not, measuring myself against others, never deciding what *I* actually want.

But I'm also too self-aware for my own good. I spend so much time analyzing myself that I forget to just *live* — to have fun, to be present, to enjoy the moment without turning it into a lesson.

That changes now.

---

## Rules for My 20th Year

- I don't go back to what broke me
- I don't depend on others for stability
- I don't compare my life with others
- I don't stop — I only slow down

---

## Non-Negotiables

- Daily workout
- Daily cybersecurity work
- No dopamine traps controlling me

---

## Focus

- Build skill
- Build discipline
- Build a strong, small circle

---

## What I Did Wrong This Year

- Gave my ex a re-entry into my life
- Became dependent on others for my happiness
- Constantly compared my growth to others'
- Didn't value my friendships enough
- Failed to stay consistent
- Stopped working out

---

## The Goal by 21

Become job-ready and mentally strong — and actually enjoy the journey while getting there.
