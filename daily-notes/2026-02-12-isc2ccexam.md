## **Domain 5: Security Operations**

* **Monitoring** â€“ Continuous visibility into systems and logs to detect anomalies early.
* **SIEM (Security Information and Event Management)** â€“ Centralized platform that collects, correlates, and alerts on log data from across the environment. Examples: Splunk, Microsoft Sentinel, IBM QRadar.
* **Vulnerability Management** â€“ Ongoing cycle of scanning, finding, prioritizing, and patching weaknesses before attackers exploit them.
* **Hardening** â€“ Reduce attack surface by disabling unnecessary services, closing unused ports, removing default credentials, and applying secure configurations (CIS Benchmarks are a good reference).
* **SOC (Security Operations Center)** â€“ Dedicated team responsible for real-time monitoring, threat detection, and incident response.
* **Patch Management** â€“ Process of regularly applying updates to OS, software, and firmware to fix known vulnerabilities. Unpatched systems are one of the most common attack vectors.
* **Threat Intelligence** â€“ Collecting and analyzing data about current threats, attacker tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) to stay ahead of adversaries. Feeds into SIEM and detection rules.
* **Log Management** â€“ Collecting, storing, and reviewing logs from systems, applications, and network devices. Logs are critical for forensic investigations and compliance.
* **Change Management** â€“ Controlled process for making changes to IT systems so that new changes don't introduce new vulnerabilities or misconfigurations.
* **Asset Management** â€“ Knowing what devices, software, and data exist in your environment. You can't protect what you don't know about.
* **Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)** â€“ Tools deployed on endpoints (laptops, servers) to detect, investigate, and respond to threats in real time. Examples: CrowdStrike Falcon, SentinelOne.
* **Threat Hunting** â€“ Proactive approach where analysts manually search through data to find hidden threats that automated tools may have missed. Goes beyond alert-driven detection.
* **Forensics & Evidence Handling** â€“ Collecting and preserving digital evidence after an incident in a forensically sound manner (chain of custody) so it can be used for investigation or legal proceedings.
* **Configuration Management** â€“ Ensuring systems are consistently configured according to approved baselines and that unauthorized changes are detected quickly.
